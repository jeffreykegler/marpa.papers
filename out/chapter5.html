An autotools replacement

Outline: The replacement could provide autoconf's functionality, or a subset thereof, but with a friendlier, Marpa-powered, syntax.

Jeffrey's thoughts:

  • Look at Alberto Sim√µes's Config::AutoConf, an excellent albeit partial re-implementation of autoconf.

  • Do not try to be compatible with files in the old autoconf syntax, certainly not at first. That is, consider writing the replacement as a front end, translating from your friendlier syntax into the autotools files. That will have two advantages: firstly, you won't have to re-implement the semantics, which would be very difficult; and secondly, potential users will be more comfortable if they have the option of simply returning to traditional autotools, taking their work with them.

Marpa Google Group link.

A Diagnostic C Parser

Outline: Marpa knows C syntax far better than recursive descent- or LALR-style parsers ever possibly could. Leverage this knowledge to pinpoint syntax errors.

Jeffrey's thoughts:

  • Starting point would be Jean-Damien Durands's excellent MarpaX::Languages::C::AST, a very carefully written, standards-aware, parser.

  • This would be a highly useful tool. Even one project, if it was large enough, could justify the effort to create it.
